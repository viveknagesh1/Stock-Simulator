Stocks Made Easy

Why

Have you thought about buying stock in a certain company but just didn’t have the money to make a trade? Or perhaps you heard news about a company and thought to yourself that the stock price was poised to rise? Thanks to virtual stock exchange technology, stock market simulators (aka stock market games) let you invest in stocks, make trades and track the results — all without risking a penny—are as close as your keyboard or cell phone.

How

A stock market simulator is a program or application that attempts to reproduce or duplicate some or all the features of a live stock market on a computer so that a player may practice trading stocks without financial risk. This is done by the manipulation of virtual money and investment positions that behave in a manner similar to the real markets.[1] New investors can practice making (or losing) fortunes time and time again before actually committing financially.






Instructions

A user can create a new game room or join a pre-existing game. Each game has access to the stock exchange where all the stocks are shown, here the users can do a real time analysis of the stocks. Once they have decided the stocks they want to invest in, they can go to the trade option where they are allowed to trade in stocks by multiple means, short selling and other such techniques may or may not be allowed depending on the game settings which are decided by the owner. The user can track his gains and losses of his investments in the portfolio section. The section will also have the users stock history.
